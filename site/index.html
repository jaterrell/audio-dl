High-quality audio

A fast yt-dlp frontend that pulls pristine audio — or video — from YouTube, SoundCloud, and a thousand other sites, then hands it to ffmpeg for exactly the format you asked for.

One flag decides everything.

    

      --format is the single switch: audio formats extract the best
      stream, mp4 merges bestvideo+bestaudio. No mode flags to remember.
    

    

      

        
⚡︎

        
Parallel everything

        
Run URLs concurrently with -j4 and split each track into
        parallel fragments with --fragments 8. Saturate the pipe, not your patience.

      

      

        
♫

        
Playlists & batches

        
Pass one URL or twenty. --playlist grabs the whole thing;
        files that already exist are skipped unless you --force.

      

      

        
✦

        
Art & metadata baked in

        
Cover art and tags are embedded automatically on formats that support
        them — your library looks right the moment the download lands.

      

      

        
🔗

        
URL hygiene

        
Tracking junk is stripped from YouTube, SoundCloud, and Bunny Stream
        links, while auth params like secret_token are carefully preserved.

      

      

        
🔒

        
Gated content, your keys

        
--cookies-from-browser, Netscape cookie files, and
        SoundCloud OAuth via --sc-auth. Credentials stay in the CLI, never the UI.

      

      

        
⌘

        
Self-contained macOS app

        
The .app bundle ships its own ffmpeg — download, right-click →
        Open, and it just works. No Homebrew required.

      

    

  


  
  

    

      
A web UI that plays as good as it looks.

      

        audio-dl-ui spins up a local web app: paste URLs, watch live
        progress over server-sent events, cancel jobs, and reveal finished files in
        Finder. Light and dark themes, album-art-tinted Now Playing, CSRF-protected
        and loopback-only by default.

        audio-dl is a thin, content-agnostic wrapper around yt-dlp for personal,
        non-commercial use: archiving what you own or created, saving what's offered
